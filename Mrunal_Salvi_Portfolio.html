CASE STUDY / 01

Supply Chain Network Analysis

SQL / PostgreSQL Python Power BI Kepler.gl
30%
Cost Reduction

The Problem

A leading lubricant manufacturer was experiencing persistent late deliveries across multiple regions. Operations leadership had diagnosed a vehicle capacity shortage and was preparing to commission a fleet expansion. The analysis needed to validate — or challenge — that assumption before resources were committed.

My Approach

Rather than accepting the capacity framing, I ran SQL-based EDA on routing records, delivery timestamps and vehicle utilisation logs covering 800 national fleet vehicles and 40 regional distribution nodes. I analysed performance at node level rather than fleet level — the distinction that made the real constraint visible. Geospatial visualisation in Kepler.gl mapped the concentration of delays geographically.

Key Findings

  • Vehicles were running below capacity in most regions — the fleet was not undersized
  • Delays were concentrated at specific distribution nodes, not distributed across the fleet
  • Network design had not been reviewed since regional expansion — nodes were handling volumes they were never configured for

CASE STUDY / 02

National SaaS Platform Rollout

Requirements Engineering JIRA UAT Power BI
100%
Adoption Rate

The Problem

A location intelligence SaaS platform needed national deployment across 17 states to 70 regional managers and a central senior leadership team. Both groups had fundamentally different requirements — and neither initially understood the other's constraints.

My Approach

I separated the requirements workshops deliberately. Regional managers first, senior stakeholders second. Mixed sessions produced noise rather than requirements. I documented functional and non-functional specifications from each group independently, mapped the conflicts, and presented a structured resolution to both parties before any technical work began.

Key Findings

  • Requirements conflicts were structural — regional granularity vs governance outputs — not resolvable by compromise
  • Separation of workshop audiences was critical to surfacing real requirements
  • Early KPI governance framework prevented post-deployment disputes about what success looked like

CASE STUDY / 03

Healthcare Risk Prediction Model

Python / Scikit-Learn Clinical Requirements Healthcare Analytics
82%
Model Accuracy

The Problem

A healthcare technology client needed to identify high-risk Type 2 Diabetes patients early enough to enable preventive intervention. Manual clinical assessment processes were too slow and inconsistent at scale across a monitored patient population.

My Approach

I worked directly with clinical teams to translate medical domain requirements into analytical specifications the engineering team could build against. The harder challenge was the language gap between how clinicians described patient risk and what the data infrastructure could support. I bridged both groups throughout, validating model outputs against clinical intervention thresholds before deployment.

Key Findings

  • 82% prediction accuracy achieved on monitored patient dataset using classification model
  • Clinical requirements translation was the critical success factor — not model complexity
  • Post-deployment benefit tracking confirmed early intervention enabled for 700 high-risk patients
